Grapefruit Fresh Market Hybrids
Grapefruit Breeding aims to be one of the world's leading programs in the development, cultivation, and marketing of high-quality grapefruit varieties. We invest intensively in research and development to provide solutions to growers and consumers in numerous countries around the world. In particular, we focus on breeding improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different climates and markets globally.
Currently, emphasis is placed on combining resistance to Citrus Canker and Citrus Tristeza Virus (CTV) with other soil-borne diseases into superior grapefruit breeding lines and hybrids. Moreover, comprehensive efforts are being undertaken to develop varieties resistant to Huanglongbing (HLB), also known as Citrus Greening Disease.
In terms of horticultural traits, breeding focuses on early fruiting, different fruit sizes, enhanced juiciness, deep red fruit color, superior flavor, peel thickness, extended shelf life, and adaptability to high/low temperatures. We seek combinations of quality features and disease resistance in various grapefruit types, including red, white, and pink varieties.
Grapefruit breeding programs focus on three main segments: Greenhouse Grapefruits, Open-field Indeterminate Grapefruits, Bush Type Open-field Grapefruits
Greenhouse Grapefruit Breeding Program
This breeding is aimed at the Spanish and Turkish markets. It focuses on both single and clustered fruit production, round fruit shape, and resistance to Citrus Canker, CTV, and Nematodes. Some hybrids may also include additional resistances, such as Phytophthora and Greasy Spot.
Open-field Indeterminate
Grapefruits
This breeding program is designed for Brazilian, Moroccan, and Turkish markets. The main focus is on large, round fruits (weighing 300 grams or more) with enhanced resistance to Citrus Canker, CTV, and Nematodes. In the near future, resistance to Alternaria Brown Spot will be incorporated as a fundamental trait in our cultivars.
Open-field Bush Type
Grapefruits
This breeding program targets Mediterranean markets. We develop round and elongated grapefruit varieties, including standard sizes (140–180 grams) and larger sizes (minimum 180 grams up to 400 grams and more). Our hybrids integrate resistance to Citrus Canker, CTV, and Nematodes as core traits. However, some hybrids may also include additional resistances, such as Phytophthora and Greasy Spot.
Grapefruit breeding operates through international and local distributors worldwide, ensuring that our improved hybrids reach farmers and consumers efficiently.
